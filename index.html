Main Content Title

Web accessibility refers to the deliberate design and development of websites, tools, and technologies that ensure usability for individuals with disabilities. It entails enabling people to perceive, understand, navigate, and interact with the web content and also facilitates their active participation and contribution to the online environment.

Section Heading

In software engineering, a software design pattern signifies a reusable and general solution for addressing recurring issues in software design within a particular context. It doesn't represent a finalized design directly translatable into source or machine code. Instead, it serves as a blueprint or guideline for resolving problems applicable across various scenarios. These patterns encapsulate standardized and effective practices enabling programmers to address common challenges when designing applications or systems.

Ensuring web accessibility guarantees a smooth experience for all website visitors, irrespective of their abilities. Regrettably, numerous websites fail to adhere to recommended web accessibility standards and practices, leading to challenges for users with disabilities when navigating these sites.
